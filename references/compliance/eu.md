# EU Compliance Deep-Dive

> **⚠️ DISCLAIMER:** Laws change. This reflects early 2026 status. Always verify
> with local legal counsel before relying on any specific requirement.

## Data Protection: GDPR
- Lawful basis: Consent, contract, legitimate interest, legal obligation, vital interest, public task
- DPO: Mandatory if large-scale processing of special categories or systematic monitoring
- DPIA: Required for high-risk processing (profiling, large-scale special data, systematic monitoring)
- Breach notification: 72 hours to supervisory authority. "Without undue delay" to individuals if high risk.
- Cross-border: Adequacy decisions (UK, Japan, Korea, etc.) or SCCs or BCRs
- Right to be forgotten: Delete on request unless legal obligation to retain
- Data portability: Provide data in structured, machine-readable format
- Children: Consent age 16 (member states can lower to 13). Parental consent below threshold.
- Penalties: Up to 4% of global annual turnover or €20M, whichever is higher
- Representative: Non-EU companies serving EU users must appoint an EU representative

## Digital Services Act (DSA)
- Applies to: All intermediary services accessible in EU
- Obligations scale with size: Micro/small → Medium → Large → VLOP/VLOSE (45M+ EU users)
- Content moderation: Transparent policies, clear notice-and-action, appeal mechanism
- Transparency: Semi-annual transparency reports (annual for non-large)
- Risk assessments: VLOPs must assess systemic risks (illegal content, fundamental rights, elections)
- Independent audits: VLOPs annually audited by independent body
- Recommender systems: Users must have option for non-profiling-based recommendations

## Digital Markets Act (DMA)
- Applies to: Gatekeepers (large platforms: >€7.5B turnover OR >€75B market cap AND >45M EU users)
- Obligations: Interoperability, no self-preferencing, data portability, sideloading allowed
- If you're not a gatekeeper: DMA doesn't apply directly, but shapes the ecosystem you operate in

## AI Act (effective 2025-2027, phased)
- Prohibited: Social scoring, real-time biometric surveillance (exceptions), subliminal manipulation
- High-risk: Employment decisions, credit scoring, education, healthcare, law enforcement
  → Conformity assessment, risk management, human oversight, transparency, accuracy
- Limited risk: Chatbots, deepfakes → Transparency obligations (disclose AI interaction)
- Minimal risk: Most AI applications → No specific obligations, voluntary codes of practice

## Payments: PSD2/PSD3
- SCA: Strong Customer Authentication for electronic payments (3DS for cards)
- Open banking: Third-party access to payment accounts with user consent (AISP/PISP)
- E-money: EMD2 for electronic money issuers (wallets)
- SEPA: Standardized euro transfers across EU

## Tax
- VAT: Standard rates 17-27% by country. Digital services: VAT in customer's country.
- VAT OSS (One-Stop Shop): Single registration for cross-border B2C digital services
- VAT threshold: €10,000/year in cross-border B2C sales (below = charge home country rate)
- Country-specific: France DST (3%), Italy DST (3%), Spain DST (3%) on digital advertising revenue

## Employment
- Working Time Directive: Max 48 hrs/week average. Min 4 weeks paid annual leave.
- Minimum vacation: 20 days/year minimum (most countries offer 25-30)
- Notice periods: Vary by country and tenure. Germany: 1-7 months. France: 1-3 months.
- Works councils: Mandatory in many countries for certain employee thresholds
- GDPR for employees: Employee data processing needs lawful basis too.
- Country-specific: Very significant variation. ALWAYS get local legal counsel.
