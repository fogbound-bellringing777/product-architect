# UK Compliance Deep-Dive

> **⚠️ DISCLAIMER:** Laws change. This reflects early 2026 status. Always verify
> with local legal counsel before relying on any specific requirement.

## Data Protection: UK GDPR + DPA 2018
- Post-Brexit: Mirrors EU GDPR but enforced by ICO (Information Commissioner's Office)
- UK adequacy: EU has granted adequacy to UK (data can flow freely EU↔UK for now)
- Differences: Some flexibility on research, AI/automated decisions, legitimate interest balancing
- Children: Age of consent for data processing is 13 (vs. 16 in EU GDPR default)
- Breach notification: 72 hours to ICO. "Without undue delay" to individuals if high risk.
- Penalties: Up to 4% of global annual turnover or £17.5M, whichever is higher
- DPDI Bill: Potential reforms to reduce burden on small businesses (monitor status)

## Online Safety Act 2023
- Applies to: User-to-user services and search services accessible in UK
- Illegal content duty: Remove illegal content when alerted or should have known
- Children's safety: Age verification/estimation for services likely accessed by children
- Transparency: Report moderation practices, respond to Ofcom information requests
- Ofcom: Regulator with enforcement powers including fines up to £18M or 10% of turnover

## Payments: FCA Regulation
- FCA authorization: Required for payment services, e-money, consumer credit
- Open Banking: CMA Order + PSD2 equivalent. OBIE (Open Banking Implementation Entity) standards.
- APP fraud: Mandatory reimbursement for authorized push payment fraud (from Oct 2024)
- Consumer Duty: FCA's consumer outcomes framework — products must deliver good outcomes

## Tax
- Corporation tax: 25% (profits >£250K), 19% (profits <£50K), marginal relief between
- VAT: 20% standard rate. Registration threshold: £85,000/year (mandatory above)
- PAYE: Employer deducts income tax + NI from employee salary
- National Insurance: Employer pays 13.8% above £9,100/year per employee
- R&D tax relief: SME scheme (up to 186% deduction) or RDEC (20% credit for larger companies)
- Digital Services Tax: 2% on revenues from UK users of search, social media, online marketplaces (>£500M global, >£25M UK)

## Employment
- Employment Rights Act 1996: Unfair dismissal protection after 2 years service
- National Minimum Wage: £11.44/hour (23+), lower bands for younger workers (April 2024)
- Working Time Regulations: 48-hour max week (opt-out available), 28 days paid leave (including bank holidays)
- Notice periods: Statutory minimum 1 week per year of service (up to 12 weeks)
- Auto-enrolment pension: Mandatory. Employer minimum 3%, employee minimum 5%.
- Gender pay gap reporting: Mandatory for 250+ employees.
- Modern Slavery Act: Annual statement for companies with £36M+ turnover.
- IR35: Off-payroll working rules for contractors. Medium/large companies assess status.
