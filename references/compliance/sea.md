# Southeast Asia Compliance Deep-Dive

> **⚠️ DISCLAIMER:** Laws change. This reflects early 2026 status. Always verify
> with local legal counsel before relying on any specific requirement.

## Singapore
- PDPA: Consent-based. DPO mandatory. Breach notification <3 days to PDPC if significant.
- Payments: MAS (Monetary Authority of Singapore) licensing — Payment Services Act 2019.
  License types: Standard (any 1 service), Major (2+ services or thresholds exceeded)
- Tax: Corporate 17%. GST 9%. No capital gains tax. Tax treaties with 90+ countries.
- Employment: Fair Consideration Framework for hiring (Singaporeans first for EP holders).
  MOM governs employment passes. Minimum rest days, CPF contributions mandatory.
- E-commerce: CCCS (Competition and Consumer Commission) enforces fair trading.

## Indonesia
- PDP Law 2022: GDPR-inspired. Consent, DPO, breach notification 3×24 hours.
  Data localization: Certain categories must be stored in Indonesia.
- Payments: OJK licenses for fintech. GoPay, OVO, Dana, ShopeePay dominant.
  QRIS: National QR standard for payments (like India's UPI QR).
- Tax: Corporate 22%. VAT 11%. Digital services by non-residents: 11% VAT.
- Employment: Omnibus Law on Job Creation (2023). Minimum wage varies by province.
  Severance: Substantial — up to 9 months salary for long-tenure employees.
- E-commerce: GR 80/2019 on e-commerce. BRTI/Kominfo for internet content regulation.

## Thailand
- PDPA (2022, enforced 2023): Similar to GDPR. Consent, DPO, breach notification 72 hours.
- Payments: PromptPay (national payment scheme, like UPI). BOT licenses for fintech.
- Tax: Corporate 20%. VAT 7%. E-services VAT: 7% on non-resident digital services.
- Employment: Labor Protection Act. Minimum wage varies by province (300-400 THB/day).
  Severance: 30-400 days based on tenure.
- Foreign Business Act: Certain businesses restricted to Thai nationals. Check List 1-3.

## Vietnam
- Cybersecurity Law 2018 + PDP Decree 2023: Data localization for certain categories.
  User data stored in Vietnam for minimum period. DPO required for large-scale processing.
- Payments: NAPAS (national payment network). MoMo, ZaloPay dominant mobile wallets.
  SBV (State Bank of Vietnam) licenses for payment intermediaries.
- Tax: Corporate 20%. VAT 8-10%. Foreign contractor tax on cross-border digital services.
- Employment: Labor Code 2019. Minimum wage varies by region (I-IV).
  Social insurance: Mandatory (employer 17.5%, employee 8%).
- E-commerce: Decree 52/2013, amended. Registration required for e-commerce platforms.

## Philippines
- DPA 2012: NPC (National Privacy Commission) enforces. DPO mandatory.
  Breach notification within 72 hours to NPC + affected individuals.
- Payments: BSP (Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas) regulates. GCash, Maya dominant.
  InstaPay and PESONet for fund transfers.
- Tax: Corporate 25% (20% for SMEs with net taxable income ≤₱5M and assets ≤₱100M).
  VAT 12%. Withholding tax on digital services.
- Employment: Labor Code. Security of tenure (hard to terminate). 13th month pay mandatory.
  Minimum wage: Regional (₱570-610/day in NCR). SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG mandatory.

## Malaysia
- PDPA 2010: Commissioner enforces. Consent, access, correction rights. No data localization.
  Breach notification: Proposed in amendments (monitor status).
- Payments: BNM (Bank Negara Malaysia) regulates. DuitNow QR (national QR standard).
  Touch 'n Go eWallet, GrabPay, Boost dominant.
- Tax: Corporate 24%. SST (Sales and Service Tax) 6-10%. Digital service tax 6% on non-residents.
- Employment: Employment Act 1955 (amended 2022). Min wage RM1,500/month.
  Reduced max working hours to 45/week. Foreign worker levy.
