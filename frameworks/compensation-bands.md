# Compensation & Salary Bands Framework

> **⚠️ DISCLAIMER:** Salary data is approximate as of early 2026 and will age.
> Always benchmark against current market data before making offers.
> See [DISCLAIMER.md](../references/DISCLAIMER.md).

## Compensation Philosophy

```
PRINCIPLES:
1. Pay for VALUE delivered, not just time spent
2. Benchmark against MARKET, not just internal equity (but maintain internal equity too)
3. TRANSPARENCY: Every employee knows their band, where they sit, and how to move up
4. TOTAL COMPENSATION: Base + Equity + Benefits + Perks = Total Package
5. GEOGRAPHY-AWARE: Adjust for cost of living, not cost of labor (pay for role, not location — with geographic modifier)

COMPENSATION FORMULA:
Total Comp = Base Salary + Variable/Bonus + Equity (annualized) + Benefits Value

BAND PLACEMENT:
- P25: Below market (only acceptable for equity-heavy early startups)
- P50: Market rate (adequate retention for most roles)
- P75: Competitive (strong retention, attracts experienced talent)
- P90: Premium (for critical roles, niche skills, or aggressive hiring markets)
```

### COMPENSATION REVIEW PROCESS

```
ANNUAL REVIEW CYCLE:
1. Market benchmarking (January): Update bands using Levels.fyi, Glassdoor, AmbitionBox, Pave, Radford
2. Budget allocation (February): Finance approves total compensation increase budget (typically 8-15% of payroll)
3. Manager recommendations (March): Each manager submits recommendations per direct report
4. Calibration (March): Leadership calibrates across teams for fairness
5. Approval (April): CEO/CFO final approval
6. Communication (April): Managers communicate to direct reports in 1:1
7. Effective date: April 1 (India FY start) or January 1 (US calendar year)

MID-YEAR ADJUSTMENTS (exceptional only):
- Counter-offers (when employee has competing offer)
- Promotion (immediate adjustment to new band)
- Market correction (role has moved significantly since last review)
- Retention (critical employee at high flight risk)

SALARY REVIEW CRITERIA:
| Factor | Weight | How Measured |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Performance rating | 40% | Review cycle output |
| Market positioning | 25% | Band placement vs. market data |
| Internal equity | 20% | Comparison to peers at same level |
| Skills growth | 15% | New skills, certifications, scope expansion |
```

## SALARY BAND MAINTENANCE PROCESS

```
THE PROBLEM: Salary data ages. Bands from 2026 may be 10-20% wrong by 2028.
Especially in tech where comp inflation has been 8-15% annually in India.

QUARTERLY: PULSE CHECK
□ Check 2-3 recent offers made (accepted and rejected)
□ Are we losing candidates on comp? Track "comp was the reason" in rejection data.
□ Are we getting "this is below my current" in negotiations? → Bands may be stale.
□ Quick scan: Levels.fyi, AmbitionBox, Glassdoor for your top 3 roles.
□ Decision: Bands still accurate? Flag roles that feel off.

ANNUALLY: FULL REBENCHMARK
□ Month 1: Collect data
  Sources: Levels.fyi, Glassdoor, AmbitionBox, Pave, Radford, Mercer
  Method: For each role × level, collect 10+ data points from 3+ sources
  Focus: Your top 10 roles by headcount (covers 80%+ of employees)
□ Month 2: Analyze and propose
  Compare current bands to fresh market data
  Identify: Which bands are below market? Above? On target?
  Propose adjusted bands with cost impact analysis
  Calculate: "If we adjust all below-market employees, total cost = ₹X"
□ Month 3: Approve and communicate
  Finance approves budget impact
  Leadership approves band changes
  Managers communicate to affected employees in 1:1s
  HR updates offer templates and job postings

TRIGGER-BASED UPDATES (don't wait for annual cycle):
□ Lost 3+ candidates in same role on comp → Immediate band review for that role
□ Key competitor raises comp publicly → Assess impact on your hiring
□ Market event (funding boom, layoff wave) → Check if supply/demand shifted
□ New geography: Entering a new city/country → Research local bands before first hire
□ Inflation spike: If CPI increases >8% YoY → Review all bands for adequacy

DATA HYGIENE:
□ Every comp data point tagged with: source, date collected, geography, company stage
□ Remove data older than 18 months from active benchmarking (use for trend only)
□ Weight recent data (last 6 months) 2x vs. older data
□ Distinguish: Startup comp (equity-heavy) vs. big tech (cash-heavy) vs. services (lower)
□ Account for: Total comp (base + bonus + equity) not just base salary

AUTOMATION OPPORTUNITIES:
⚡ Set Google Alerts for "[your company] salary" and "tech salary india 2026"
⚡ Quarterly Levels.fyi/Glassdoor scrape for your top 10 roles (manual, 2 hours)
⚡ Compensation management tools: Pave, Ravio, Figures — automate benchmarking
⚡ Track offer acceptance rate by role — declining rate = early warning signal
```
